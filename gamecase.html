Game Case
The best way to track your video game collection.
Mobile
Product Design

Game Case is a project I started in 2018 with a developer colleague of mine. The idea was simple - we'd seen the success of apps that help users track their TV-watching habits, and as avid gamers ourselves, thought the same principles could be applied to video game collections.


Over the course of a few short days, we developed a prototype that we could use to test - and hopefully prove - the concept. Whilst my colleague did the development, I was responsible for the user experience, visual design, and branding of the product. I also built an interactive prototype for use to use for early user-acceptance testing.

Early Designs

User Flow

Though not a complex product, I felt it was important to identify a core use-case to begin building the app upon. The most basic version of our app would require a sign-up, some basic data from the user, and a database that can be used to either find new games, or add old ones to your collection.


This simple set of requirements allowed us to quickly put together a minimum viable prototype, which you'll see below.



Early Concepts

The layouts and patterns of the app were initially based closely on other apps I found during competitor analysis.


Popular apps like TVTime or iTV had already established patterns that matched our use case.
